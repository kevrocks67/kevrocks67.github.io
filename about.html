I am an IT Specialist at the Columbia Secondary School in New York. Working at the same school I graduated from and in the same IT department I volunteered at for almost 2 years is great. I work on everything from managing the G Suite domain to repairing damaged computers to running lights (DMX) and AV equipment for events. I run, maintain, and document as well as look for ways to improve our teams efficiency. I love working in this field.

I am also a student at the John Jay College of Criminal Justice where I am working on a Bachelors in Computer Science and Information Security. I have also had the chance to take part in several cybercrime classes where I have learned about the criminology and laws behind the malicious actors we fight today.

I have been around technology and people who love technology for my entire life. People in my family worked in or loved technology. I would always watch them repair and build computers, so I picked up some of those skills. By the age of 11, I had the knowledge to build my own computer. At the time, I was extremely ambitious in attempting to find ways to make a computer from scratch. When I say scratch, I mean from absolutely nothing. I wanted to make the perfect computer with the perfect motherboard since it seemed you could never get exactly what you wanted with the big box companies. Now, I did not end up doing this - of course, but that does not really matter because what was important was the interest. It threw me into the world of electronics and computer engineering. Within the next 2 years, I learned arduino, basic breadboarding, and circuit theory. This world was fascinating to me and led me to a mindset of always digging deeper to understand how things work. This mindset is what has helped me become who I am today.

I spend my time watching lectures, talks, and youtube videos to learn new technological concepts. I also read books and as I learn these new concepts I try to create small programming projects or do small practice labs to cement in the knowledge. I also occasionally will write a blog post about it. When im not learning, I spend my time helping out people in and moderating/administering the Engineer Man and IT Career Questions communities on Discord and Youtube. In both communities, we seek to help people get into the tech field and network with other people already in the field. We help them find resources to learn things and solve any technological issues they may be facing. The Engineer Man community is focused on primarily programming, while the ITCQ community is focused on the IT field directly.

I became really interested in information security in early 2016 after reading an old book on my dad's shelf called "Hacking Exposed". As I dug deeper into the field and started playing around with things, I realized I did not fully understand many things and decided I needed (and wanted) to learn more about the fundamentals of IT infrastucture and administration.
By getting a good understanding of how a network functions or how an enterprise environment goes together, I am finding that I am better able to understand the security concepts and why certain defenses may or may not work. I know that this time is well spent and will lead to me becoming a much better security expert than I could have ever been had I not gone down this path.
